SANTA TERESA NATIONAL PARK

what is the santa teresa national park?

Santa Teresa is a famous national park that has 3,000 hectares of extension,4 in which there are innumerable species of native and exotic flora, including more than 2 million trees.

Information about santa teresa national park

In 1762 the Portuguese, foreseeing a new conflict with Spain, decided to fortify the point called Castillos Chicos. Thus began the work of the Fortress of Santa Teresa. In 1825 it passed into the hands of the easterners, and remained abandoned for a long time, until it was rediscovered by the historian Horacio Arredondo, who began the efforts for its reconstruction in 1928
