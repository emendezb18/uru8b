Gorriti Island is 1.7 km long, 900 m wide at its widest part and 160 m wide at its narrowest part, and has 71 ha.1 It is within the Río de la Plata, near Its eastern center is an island surrounded by Uruguayan maritime waters of the Río de la Plata

Information about gorriti island

The island was recognized in 1516 by Juan Díaz de Solís; later, it was sighted in 1527 by Diego García de Moguer, who named it Isla de las Palmas; it was also sighted by Sebastián Gaboto. It was baptized in homage to the Spanish commander Francisco Gorriti, chief of Montevideo in the first half of the 18th century, who had a fortification built there to defend the Río de la Plata against the advancing Portuguese.
